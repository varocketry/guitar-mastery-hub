## üìñ SECTION 1: THE NATURAL MINOR SCALE

### What Is the Natural Minor Scale?

**Natural minor:** The fundamental minor scale, built with a different pattern of whole and half steps than major.

**Major scale formula (review):**  
**W - W - H - W - W - W - H**

**Natural minor scale formula:**  
**W - H - W - W - H - W - W**

**Notice the difference:**
- Major: Half steps between 3-4 and 7-8
- Minor: Half steps between 2-3 and 5-6

**This creates a completely different sound.**

---

### Building A Natural Minor Scale

**Start with A (the easiest minor scale - no sharps or flats)**

```
A ‚Üí B = 2 frets (W) ‚úì
B ‚Üí C = 1 fret (H) ‚úì
C ‚Üí D = 2 frets (W) ‚úì
D ‚Üí E = 2 frets (W) ‚úì
E ‚Üí F = 1 fret (H) ‚úì
F ‚Üí G = 2 frets (W) ‚úì
G ‚Üí A = 2 frets (W) ‚úì

Formula: W - H - W - W - H - W - W ‚úì

A Natural Minor Scale: A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A
```

**No sharps, no flats - just like C major!**

**This is WHY A minor is the "relative minor" of C major.**

---

### The Relative Minor Relationship

**Every major scale has a relative minor that shares the same notes (same key signature).**

**C Major:** C - D - E - F - G - A - B - C  
**A Natural Minor:** A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A

**Same notes, different starting point!**

**C major starts on C (sounds major, bright)**  
**A minor starts on A (sounds minor, dark)**

**The starting note (tonic) determines whether it sounds major or minor.**

---

### Relative Minor Formula

**To find the relative minor of any major key:**

**Go to the 6th scale degree.**

**Examples:**

**C major scale:** C - D - E - F - G - **A** - B - C  
6th degree = A  
**Relative minor: A minor**

**G major scale:** G - A - B - C - D - **E** - F# - G  
6th degree = E  
**Relative minor: E minor**

**D major scale:** D - E - F# - G - A - **B** - C# - D  
6th degree = B  
**Relative minor: B minor**

**This relationship is fundamental to all Western music.**

---

### Building Natural Minor Scales from the Formula

**E Natural Minor:**

```
E ‚Üí F# = W
F# ‚Üí G = H (F# to G is only 1 fret)
G ‚Üí A = W
A ‚Üí B = W
B ‚Üí C = H (B to C is natural half step)
C ‚Üí D = W
D ‚Üí E = W

E Natural Minor: E - F# - G - A - B - C - D - E
```

**Key signature: 1 sharp (F#)** - same as G major!

---

**D Natural Minor:**

```
D ‚Üí E = W
E ‚Üí F = H
F ‚Üí G = W
G ‚Üí A = W
A ‚Üí Bb = H (A to B is whole step, need half)
Bb ‚Üí C = W
C ‚Üí D = W

D Natural Minor: D - E - F - G - A - Bb - C - D
```

**Key signature: 1 flat (Bb)** - same as F major!

---

### The Sound of Natural Minor

**Natural minor sounds:**
- Sad, melancholic
- Introspective, emotional
- Somber, serious

**Used extensively in:**

## üìñ SECTION 2: THE HARMONIC MINOR SCALE

### Why We Need Harmonic Minor

**Problem with natural minor:**

In major keys, the V chord (dominant) creates strong tension that resolves to I.

**C major: G major (V) ‚Üí C major (I)** = Strong resolution

**But in natural minor:**

**A minor: E minor (v) ‚Üí A minor (i)** = Weak resolution!

**Why?** The E minor chord doesn't have the leading tone (G#) that pulls strongly to A.

**Solution: Raise the 7th scale degree to create a leading tone.**

**This creates the harmonic minor scale.**

---

### Building the Harmonic Minor Scale

**Harmonic minor formula:**

**Start with natural minor, then raise the 7th degree by one half step.**

**A Natural Minor:** A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A  
**A Harmonic Minor:** A - B - C - D - E - F - **G#** - A

**The only difference: G becomes G#**

**Interval pattern:**  
**W - H - W - W - H - W+H - H**

**Notice:** Between the 6th and 7th degrees is now 3 half steps (W+H = "augmented 2nd")

**This creates the exotic, Middle Eastern sound of harmonic minor.**

---

### E Harmonic Minor

**E Natural Minor:** E - F# - G - A - B - C - D - E  
**E Harmonic Minor:** E - F# - G - A - B - C - **D#** - E

**Raise the 7th (D becomes D#)**

---

**D Harmonic Minor:**

**D Natural Minor:** D - E - F - G - A - Bb - C - D  
**D Harmonic Minor:** D - E - F - G - A - Bb - **C#** - D

**Raise the 7th (C becomes C#)**

---

### The Sound of Harmonic Minor

**Harmonic minor sounds:**
- Exotic, Middle Eastern
- Dramatic, theatrical
- Mysterious, mystical
- Classical, baroque
- Spooky, ominous (Halloween music!)

**The augmented 2nd interval (F to G# in A harmonic minor) creates this exotic quality.**

**Used extensively in:**
- Classical music (Bach, Mozart, Beethoven)
- Flamenco guitar
- Jewish and Middle Eastern music
- Metal (neoclassical metal, Yngwie Malmsteen)
- Film scores (dramatic, exotic scenes)

**Famous examples:**
- Bach's Toccata and Fugue in D minor
- Flamenco guitar solos
- Neoclassical metal shred solos
- "Hava Nagila" (traditional Jewish)

## üìñ SECTION 3: THE MELODIC MINOR SCALE

### Why We Need Melodic Minor

**Problem with harmonic minor:**

The augmented 2nd interval (F to G# in A harmonic minor) is difficult to sing.

**Singers complained: "This leap is awkward!"**

**Solution: Raise BOTH the 6th and 7th degrees (ascending only).**

**This creates the melodic minor scale.**

---

### Building the Melodic Minor Scale

**Melodic minor has TWO forms:**

**Ascending (going up):** Raise 6th and 7th degrees  
**Descending (going down):** Use natural minor (no raised notes)

**A Melodic Minor (Ascending):**

**A Natural Minor:** A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A  
**A Melodic Minor:** A - B - C - D - E - **F#** - **G#** - A

**Raise both 6th (F‚ÜíF#) and 7th (G‚ÜíG#)**

**A Melodic Minor (Descending):**

**A - G - F - E - D - C - B - A** (same as natural minor descending)

---

**Why ascending is different from descending:**

**Ascending:** Need the leading tone (G#) to pull to A, and smooth voice leading (F# avoids the augmented 2nd)

**Descending:** Don't need the leading tone when moving away from the tonic, so natural minor sounds better

**Think of it as:**
- **Ascending = melodic minor** (raised 6th and 7th)
- **Descending = natural minor** (no alterations)

---

### E Melodic Minor

**Ascending:**  
**E Natural Minor:** E - F# - G - A - B - C - D - E  
**E Melodic Minor:** E - F# - G - A - B - **C#** - **D#** - E

**Descending:**  
E - D - C - B - A - G - F# - E (natural minor)

---

**D Melodic Minor:**

**Ascending:**  
**D Natural Minor:** D - E - F - G - A - Bb - C - D  
**D Melodic Minor:** D - E - F - G - A - **B** - **C#** - D

**Descending:**  
D - C - Bb - A - G - F - E - D (natural minor)

---

### The Sound of Melodic Minor

**Melodic minor (ascending) sounds:**
- Smooth, singable
- Jazz-influenced (jazz minor)
- Sophisticated, complex
- Bright top, dark bottom (hybrid major/minor feel)

**Used extensively in:**
- Jazz (jazz minor = melodic minor ascending only)
- Classical melodies (smooth vocal lines)
- Film scores (sophisticated emotional content)
- Fusion and progressive music

**The ascending melodic minor is sometimes called "jazz minor"** because jazz musicians use it constantly for improvisation.

## üìñ SECTION 4: COMPARING ALL THREE MINOR SCALES

### A Minor - All Three Types Side by Side

**A Natural Minor:**  
A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A  
*No alterations*

**A Harmonic Minor:**  
A - B - C - D - E - F - **G#** - A  
*Raised 7th only*

**A Melodic Minor (Ascending):**  
A - B - C - D - E - **F#** - **G#** - A  
*Raised 6th and 7th*

**A Melodic Minor (Descending):**  
A - G - F - E - D - C - B - A  
*Same as natural minor*

---

### Scale Degree Comparison

```
Degree:        1   2   3   4   5   6   7   8
Natural:       A   B   C   D   E   F   G   A
Harmonic:      A   B   C   D   E   F   G#  A
Melodic (asc): A   B   C   D   E   F#  G#  A
Melodic (desc):A   G   F   E   D   C   B   A
```

**Key differences:**
- **6th degree:** Natural & harmonic use F, melodic (asc) uses F#
- **7th degree:** Natural uses G, harmonic & melodic (asc) use G#

---

### When to Use Each Minor Scale

**Natural Minor:**
- Chord progressions (i-iv-v-i)
- Sad, melancholic melodies
- Folk and traditional music
- Rock and metal rhythm playing
- **General-purpose minor scale**

**Harmonic Minor:**
- Creating strong V-i cadences (dominant resolution)
- Exotic, dramatic melodies
- Classical and baroque music
- Neoclassical metal solos
- **When you need a leading tone**

**Melodic Minor:**
- Smooth ascending melodies
- Jazz improvisation
- Classical vocal writing
- Sophisticated harmonic contexts
- **When you need singable melodies with leading tone**

## üìñ SECTION 5: MINOR SCALES ON GUITAR

### A Natural Minor - Open Position

**The easiest minor scale on guitar (no sharps/flats):**

```
E|---0---1---3---5---------------|
B|---0---1---3---5---------------|
G|---0---2----------------------|
D|---0---2---3------------------|
A|---0---2---3------------------|
E|-------------------------------|
     A   B   C   D   E   F   G   A
```

**Formula check:**
```
A ‚Üí B = W ‚úì
B ‚Üí C = H ‚úì
C ‚Üí D = W ‚úì
D ‚Üí E = W ‚úì
E ‚Üí F = H ‚úì
F ‚Üí G = W ‚úì
G ‚Üí A = W ‚úì
```

**Play this slowly (40 BPM) and listen to the sad, minor quality.**

---

### E Natural Minor - Open Position

**E Natural Minor: E - F# - G - A - B - C - D - E**

```
E|---0---2---3---5---------------|
B|---0---2---3---5---------------|
G|---0---2---4-------------------|
D|---0---2---4-------------------|
A|---0---2-----------------------|
E|---0---2---3-------------------|
     E   F#  G   A   B   C   D   E
```

**This scale has 1 sharp (F#)** - same key signature as G major.

---

### A Harmonic Minor - Open Position

**A Harmonic Minor: A - B - C - D - E - F - G# - A**

```
E|---0---1---3---4---------------|  ‚Üê G# at fret 4!
B|---0---1---3---5---------------|
G|---0---2-----------------------|
D|---0---2---3-------------------|
A|---0---2---3-------------------|
E|-------------------------------|
     A   B   C   D   E   F   G#  A
```

**Notice:** G# is fret 4 on the high E string (one fret higher than G at fret 3)

**Listen for the exotic, Middle Eastern quality between F and G#.**

---

### A Melodic Minor - Open Position (Ascending)

**A Melodic Minor (Ascending): A - B - C - D - E - F# - G# - A**

```
E|---0---1---3---4---------------|  ‚Üê G# at fret 4
B|---0---1---3---5---------------|
G|---0---2-----------------------|
D|---0---2---3-------------------|
A|---0---2---4-------------------|  ‚Üê F# at fret 4 on A string!
E|-------------------------------|
     A   B   C   D   E   F#  G#  A
```

**Notice:** Both F# and G# are raised

**Descending:** Play natural minor pattern (G-F-E-D-C-B-A)

---

### Movable Minor Scale Pattern (E String Root)

**This pattern works for ALL minor scales (natural, harmonic, melodic):**

**Natural Minor Pattern:**
```
E|--------------------------------------|
B|--------------------------------1---3-|
G|--------------------------0---2-------|
D|-------------------0---2--------------|
A|-----------0---2----------------------|
E|---ROOT----2---3----------------------|
      1     2   ‚ô≠3   4   5   ‚ô≠6  ‚ô≠7  8
```

**For harmonic minor:** Raise the 7th (move ‚ô≠7 up one fret)  
**For melodic minor (asc):** Raise both 6th and 7th (move ‚ô≠6 and ‚ô≠7 up one fret each)

**This pattern is movable:**
- Root at fret 3 = G minor
- Root at fret 5 = A minor
- Root at fret 7 = B minor
- Root at fret 8 = C minor
