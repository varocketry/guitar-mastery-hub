## üéØ DISCOVERING YOUR INNER CLASSICAL GUITARIST

**Everything you've learned so far uses a PICK.**

### What Fingerstyle Actually Is

**Strumming with a pick:**
```
[Pick hits all strings at once]
STRUM! All strings together.
Chords, rhythm, groove.
One action = one event.
```

**Fingerstyle:**
```
[Thumb plays bass note]
[Index plays string 3]
[Middle plays string 2]
[Ring plays string 1]

Four independent actions = flowing arpeggio
Sounds like harp or piano!
```

**The difference:**
- Strumming = percussive, rhythmic
- Fingerstyle = flowing, melodic

**Both are guitar. Completely different sounds.**

---

### Why This Changes Everything

**With strumming, you can play:**
- Campfire songs
- Rock rhythm parts
- Folk accompaniment  
- Chord progressions

**With fingerstyle, you can play:**
- Classical pieces (Bach, Beethoven)
- Acoustic arrangements that sound complete
- Travis picking (Chet Atkins, Tommy Emmanuel style)
- Contemporary fingerstyle (Andy McKee, Tommy Emmanuel)
- Songs that sound like melody + accompaniment from ONE guitar

**Example songs that REQUIRE fingerstyle:**
- "Landslide" - Fleetwood Mac
- "Dust in the Wind" - Kansas
- "Classical Gas" - Mason Williams
- "Blackbird" - The Beatles
- Almost all classical guitar repertoire

**You literally cannot play these songs with a pick and strumming.**

**Fingerstyle isn't just "another technique."**

**It's an entirely different way of making the guitar speak.**

### Why Starting Tempo is SO Slow

**20 BPM for initial exercises.**

**TWENTY. BEATS. PER. MINUTE.**

**That's one beat every THREE SECONDS.**

**Why so absurdly slow?**

Your brain needs time to:
1. Look at pattern notation
2. Identify which finger ("oh, index next")
3. Target which string ("oh, the B string")
4. Move that specific finger
5. Pluck with proper technique
6. Hear the result
7. Move to next finger
8. Repeat

**At 20 BPM, you can do all eight steps.**

**At 40 BPM, you're rushing and missing steps.**

**This isn't "playing music" yet. This is "building coordination."**

**Speed comes after coordination. Not before.**

## √∞≈∏‚Äú‚Äì THEORY: What Is Fingerstyle?

### Understanding Fingerstyle Playing

**Fingerstyle** = Playing guitar with individual fingers (right hand) instead of a pick or strumming.

**Key differences from strumming:**
- **Strumming:** Hit multiple strings at once
- **Fingerstyle:** Pluck individual strings separately
- **Strumming:** Creates rhythmic chord shapes
- **Fingerstyle:** Creates melodic patterns and bass lines simultaneously

**Professional fingerstyle players:**
- Tommy Emmanuel
- Andy McKee
- Chet Atkins
- Sungha Jung
- Mark Knopfler
- Lindsey Buckingham

---

### Why Learn Fingerstyle?

**1. Musical Independence**
- Play bass line and melody simultaneously
- Create fuller sound with one guitar
- No need for accompaniment

**2. Different Sound**
- Warmer, more delicate tone
- Greater dynamic range
- Perfect for ballads and gentle songs

**3. Expanded Repertoire**
- Many songs REQUIRE fingerstyle
- Classical guitar uses fingerstyle exclusively
- Opens up entire genres

**4. Professional Technique**
- All professional guitarists can fingerpick
- Essential for complete musicianship
- Develops fine motor control

## √∞≈∏≈Ω¬∏ RIGHT-HAND TECHNIQUE: Proper Positioning

### Finger Names (Classical Notation)

**Right hand fingers:**
- **p** = thumb (from Spanish "pulgar")
- **i** = index finger (from Spanish "√É¬≠ndice")
- **m** = middle finger (from Spanish "medio")
- **a** = ring finger (from Spanish "anular")

**We don't use the pinky in beginner fingerstyle.**

---

### Hand Position

**The "C" Shape:**
1. Curve fingers naturally (like holding a ball)
2. Thumb slightly forward of fingers
3. Wrist slightly arched
4. Relaxed, not tense

**String Assignments (standard):**
- **Thumb (p):** Bass strings (6, 5, 4)
- **Index (i):** 3rd string (G)
- **Middle (m):** 2nd string (B)
- **Ring (a):** 1st string (high E)

---

### Plucking Technique

**Proper pluck:**
1. Fingertip contacts string
2. Pull string slightly toward palm
3. Release (string snaps back)
4. Follow through naturally (fingers curl slightly)

**Common mistakes:**
- √¢¬ù≈í Pushing string down (toward soundboard)
- √¢¬ù≈í Plucking with nail only
- √¢¬ù≈í Lifting finger straight up
- √¢¬ù≈í Excessive motion

**Correct motion:** Small, efficient, toward palm

## √∞≈∏≈Ω¬∏ PATTERN #1: SIMPLE ARPEGGIO

### What Is an Arpeggio?

**Arpeggio** = A chord shape played one note at a time instead of all at once.

**Think of it like this:**
- **Strummed C-shape:** All notes together ‚Üí CHUNG!
- **Arpeggiated C-shape:** Same notes, but separated ‚Üí C-E-G-E-G-E...

**Classic arpeggio songs:**
- "House of the Rising Sun" (The Animals)
- "Stairway to Heaven" intro (Led Zeppelin)
- "Dust in the Wind" (Kansas)
- "Nothing Else Matters" (Metallica)

---

### Step-by-Step: Learning Pattern #1

**Stage 1: Four-Note Pattern (Days 1-2, 20 BPM)**

**Simplified version:**
```
Finger:  p   i   m   a
String:  A   G   B   E
Count:   1   2   3   4
```

**On C-shape only:**
1. Set metronome to 20 BPM
2. Thumb on beat 1 (A string)
3. Index on beat 2 (G string)
4. Middle on beat 3 (B string)
5. Ring on beat 4 (E string)
6. Repeat for 15 minutes

**Goal:** Clean, even notes. All sound the same volume.

---

**Stage 2: Six-Note Pattern (Days 3-4, 25 BPM)**

**Full pattern:**
```
Finger:  p   i   m   a   m   i
String:  A   G   B   E   B   G
Count:   1   &   2   &   3   &
```

**On C-shape:**
1. Increase metronome to 25 BPM
2. Play full six-note pattern
3. Practice 20 minutes daily
4. Pattern should flow smoothly

**Goal:** Pattern feels like one continuous motion

---

**Stage 3: Shape Changes (Days 5-6, 30 BPM)**

**Simple progression: C-shape ‚Üí G-shape**

Each shape gets one full pattern (6 notes):
```
C-shape:   p i m a m i
G-shape:   p i m a m i
```

**Instructions:**
1. Master pattern on C-shape (3 days minimum)
2. Master pattern on G-shape (2 days)
3. Practice transition slowly
4. Change shape during "p" (thumb note)

**Goal:** Smooth shape changes without breaking pattern

---

**Stage 4: Build Speed (Days 7-14, 35-50 BPM)**

Gradually increase tempo:
```
Days 7-8: 35 BPM
Days 9-10: 40 BPM
Days 11-12: 45 BPM
Days 13-14: 50 BPM (target!)
```

**Only increase tempo when flawless for 5 minutes!**

---

### Song Application: "House of the Rising Sun"

**Classic arpeggio song using Pattern #1!**

**Simplified chord shape progression:**
- Am-shape ‚Üí C-shape ‚Üí D-shape ‚Üí F-shape ‚Üí Am-shape ‚Üí E-shape ‚Üí Am-shape

## √∞≈∏≈Ω¬∏ PATTERN #2: FOLK FINGERPICKING

### What Is Folk Fingerpicking?

**Folk fingerpicking** = Alternating bass (thumb) with melody notes (fingers).

**Characteristics:**
- Steady bass rhythm (thumb)
- Melodic pickings (fingers) on top
- Used in folk, country, singer-songwriter styles

**Example songs:**
- "Dust in the Wind" (Kansas) - uses this foundation
- "Blackbird" (Beatles) - based on this pattern
- "Stairway to Heaven" intro (Led Zeppelin)

---

### Pattern #2: Basic Folk Pattern

**On C-shape:**
```
Finger:  p   i   p   m   p   i   p   m
String:  A   G   D   B   A   G   D   B
Count:   1   &   2   &   3   &   4   &
```

**Notice:** Thumb alternates between A and D strings (bass notes)

---

### Step-by-Step: Learning Pattern #2

**Stage 1: Thumb Only - Alternating Bass (Days 1-2, 20 BPM)**

**Just thumb, no fingers:**
```
Thumb:   p   p   p   p   p   p   p   p
String:  A   D   A   D   A   D   A   D
Count:   1   2   3   4   1   2   3   4
```

**On C-shape:**
1. Set metronome to 20 BPM
2. Play ONLY thumb
3. Alternate A and D strings
4. Practice 15 minutes daily for 2 days

**CRITICAL:** Master steady alternating bass before adding fingers!

**Goal:** Thumb moves independently, steady rhythm

---

**Stage 2: Add Index Finger (Days 3-4, 20 BPM)**

**Thumb + index only:**
```
Finger:  p   i   p   -   p   i   p   -
String:  A   G   D   -   A   G   D   -
Count:   1   &   2   &   3   &   4   &
```

**On C-shape:**
1. Still at 20 BPM
2. Add index finger on "&" after beat 1 and beat 3
3. Thumb maintains alternating bass
4. Practice 15 minutes daily for 2 days

**Goal:** Index doesn't disrupt thumb rhythm

---

**Stage 3: Add Middle Finger (Days 5-6, 25 BPM)**

**Full pattern:**
```
Finger:  p   i   p   m   p   i   p   m
String:  A   G   D   B   A   G   D   B
Count:   1   &   2   &   3   &   4   &
```

**On C-shape:**
1. Increase to 25 BPM
2. Add middle finger on "&" after beats 2 and 4
3. Thumb STILL maintains steady bass
4. Practice 20 minutes daily

**Goal:** All three fingers working independently

---

**Stage 4: Shape Changes (Days 7-10, 30 BPM)**

**Progression: C-shape ‚Üí G-shape ‚Üí C-shape**

Each shape gets 2 measures (8 beats):
```
C-shape:  p i p m p i p m | p i p m p i p m
G-shape:  p i p m p i p m | p i p m p i p m
```

**Instructions:**
1. Master pattern on each shape separately first
2. Change shapes on beat 1 (during thumb note)
3. Maintain pattern through change
4. Practice transition 20 times daily

---

**Stage 5: Build Speed (Days 11-14, 35-50 BPM)**

Gradual tempo increase:
```
Days 11-12: 35-40 BPM
Days 13-14: 45-50 BPM
```

**Target by end of lesson:** 50 BPM with smooth shape changes
