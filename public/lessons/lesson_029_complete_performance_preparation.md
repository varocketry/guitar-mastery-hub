## üé∏ SECTION 1: BUILDING YOUR REPERTOIRE

### Choosing Performance Songs

**Criteria for setlist songs:**

**1. Confidence Level:**
- Can play from memory (no sheet music needed)
- Confident at performance tempo (not just slow practice)
- Chord changes automatic (no thinking required)
- Multiple successful run-throughs (8/10 clean)

**2. Variety:**
- Different tempos (fast, medium, slow)
- Different feels (rock, folk, blues, ballad)
- Different techniques (strumming, fingerpicking, lead)
- Emotional range (upbeat, melancholic, energetic)

**3. Personal Connection:**
- Songs you love (passion shows)
- Songs that mean something (authentic performance)
- Songs you'd want to hear (taste matters)

---

### Setlist Construction (5-Song Set)

**The proven 15-minute structure:**

**Song 1: Strong Opener (Upbeat, Confident)**
- Start with your most solid song
- Medium tempo (not too fast)
- Establishes confidence
- Hooks audience immediately
- Example: "Wild Thing" power chords or "Horse with No Name"

**Song 2: Showcase Variety (Different from Song 1)**
- Change tempo/feel
- Show different technique
- Ballad if opener was upbeat, or vice versa
- Demonstrate range
- Example: "Blackbird" fingerstyle if opener was rock

**Song 3: Crowd Connection (Recognizable or Emotional)**
- Song people know (or deeply emotional original)
- Midpoint of set
- Build emotional connection
- Sing along possibility
- Example: "Wonderwall" or heartfelt original

**Song 4: Technical Showcase (Your Best Playing)**
- Feature your strongest skill
- Lead guitar solo, complex fingerpicking, etc.
- Demonstrate mastery
- Optional: explain briefly what makes it challenging
- Example: Complete song with lead solo

**Song 5: Strong Closer (Memorable Ending)**
- Leave them wanting more
- Upbeat and energetic
- Your second-best song (save best for opener)
- End on high note
- Example: Up-tempo crowd pleaser

---

### Practice Protocol for Setlist

**Week 1-2: Individual Song Mastery**
```
Daily practice (45-60 minutes):
- Song 1: 10 minutes (multiple run-throughs)
- Song 2: 10 minutes
- Song 3: 10 minutes
- Song 4: 10 minutes
- Song 5: 10 minutes
- Free practice: 5-10 minutes

Goal: Each song 8/10 successful from memory
```

**Week 3-4: Complete Set Practice**
```
Daily practice:
- Complete setlist start-to-finish
- No stopping between songs (simulate performance)
- Transitions matter
- Timing: 15-20 minutes total
- Run through 2x per practice session

Goal: Complete set 7/10 successful (mistakes OK, don't stop)
```

## üé∏ SECTION 2: MANAGING PERFORMANCE ANXIETY

### Understanding Anxiety

**Physical symptoms (normal):**
- Shaking hands (universal)
- Rapid heartbeat (adrenaline)
- Dry mouth (stress response)
- Sweating (nervous system activation)
- Forgetting (brain under stress)

**All performers experience this. ALL.**

---

### Technique 1: Reframing Anxiety

**Change your interpretation:**

**Old thinking:** "I'm terrified. This means something's wrong."  
**New thinking:** "I'm excited. This means I care."

**The physical sensations are identical for:**
- Anxiety/fear
- Excitement/anticipation

**Your brain decides which it is.**

**Exercise:**
Before performance, say out loud: "I'm excited to share this music."  
NOT: "I'm nervous and scared."

Language changes interpretation.

---

### Technique 2: Breathing Protocol

**The 4-7-8 Breath (from Dr. Andrew Weil):**

**Before you play:**
1. Breathe in through nose: 4 counts
2. Hold breath: 7 counts
3. Exhale through mouth: 8 counts
4. Repeat 3-4 times

**This physiologically calms nervous system.**  
Use this 5 minutes before performing.

---

### Technique 3: Preparation Ritual

**Create a consistent pre-performance routine:**

**30 minutes before:**
- Tune guitar carefully
- Play through setlist once (not performing, just reminding)
- Warm up hands (chromatic exercise)
- Hydrate

**10 minutes before:**
- Use bathroom (eliminate distraction)
- 4-7-8 breathing (3 cycles)
- Positive self-talk: "I'm ready. My music matters. Imperfection is OK."

**1 minute before:**
- Final tuning check
- Deep breath
- **Go.**

---

### Technique 4: Accept Imperfection

**The Mistake Protocol:**

**When (not if) you make a mistake:**

1. **Keep playing** (don't stop, don't apologize)
2. **Smile** (shows confidence, relaxes you)
3. **Continue to next note/chord** (forward momentum)
4. **Never verbally acknowledge** (unless catastrophic‚Äîthen laugh it off)

**What audiences remember:**
- How you recovered (resilience)
- NOT the actual mistake

**John Mayer:** *"I mess up at every show. Every single show. People think I'm perfect because I don't stop when I mess up. I smile, keep playing, and the music continues. Audiences forgive everything if you don't make it a big deal. The mistake is never the problem. Stopping is the problem."*

## üé∏ SECTION 3: RECORDING & SELF-EVALUATION

### Why Recording Is Critical

**You can't hear yourself objectively while playing.**

Recording reveals:
- Timing issues (you think are fine live)
- Technique problems (you don't notice in moment)
- Tone quality (different than you imagine)
- Strengths to emphasize (what actually sounds good)

**Every professional records themselves regularly.**

---

### Recording Protocol

**Equipment needed:**
- Smartphone (sufficient for practice recordings)
- Quiet room (reduce ambient noise)
- Consistent distance (3-5 feet from guitar)

**Weekly recording session (Week 3-8):**
```
Set up phone on stand/surface
Record complete setlist (15-20 minutes)
Do NOT listen immediately (emotions too high)
Wait 24 hours
Listen with notebook
Take notes WITHOUT judgment
```

---

### Evaluation Framework

**Listen for these (NOT in perfectionist way):**

**‚úÖ Strengths to Celebrate:**
- Which songs sound best?
- Which techniques shine?
- What moments sound musical?
- Where do you hear emotion?

**üìù Growth Areas (NOT failures):**
- Where did tempo rush/drag?
- Which transitions need work?
- Any consistent mistakes?
- Tone issues to address?

**üéØ Action Items:**
- One technical thing to improve
- One song to practice more
- One strength to emphasize in performance

**NO self-criticism. Only observation and action.**

## üé∏ SECTION 5: PLAYING WITH OTHERS

### Jam Session Basics

**What to expect:**
- Someone calls a song/progression
- Usually 12-bar blues or common progression
- Take turns soloing (rhythm when others solo, lead when your turn)
- No set structure (go with flow)

**Jam session protocol:**
1. Listen more than play (first few sessions)
2. Start simple (don't show off)
3. Respect other players' space
4. When soloing: phrase, don't fill every moment
5. When comping: support, don't overplay

---

**Musical skills:**

**Professional skills:**
- Show up on time (always)
- Come prepared (know the songs)
- Positive attitude (no ego)
- Open to feedback (growth mindset)
- Reliable equipment (tuned, working gear)

## üîß TROUBLESHOOTING

### Problem: Can't Stop Shaking When Performing

**Solutions:**
1. Reframe as excitement (not fear)
2. 4-7-8 breathing before playing
3. Play through shakiness (it lessens as you warm up)
4. More performances (exposure reduces response)
5. Accept it as normal (even pros shake)

### Problem: Forgetting Songs Mid-Performance

**Solutions:**
1. Setlist visible (no shame in having notes)
2. Practice transitions between songs
3. Have backup song (if you blank, skip to next)
4. Keep playing SOMETHING (improvise until you remember)
5. Laugh it off if necessary (audiences are forgiving)

### Problem: Perfectionism Prevents Performing

**Solutions:**
1. Set deadline (X date, you WILL perform, ready or not)
2. Redefine success (showed up and played = success)
3. Remember: imperfection connects (perfection alienates)
4. Talk to therapist if clinical anxiety (some need professional help)
5. Start with Level 1 (record and share, lowest stakes)
