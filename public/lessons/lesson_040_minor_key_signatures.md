## üìñ SECTION 1: MINOR KEY SIGNATURES EXPLAINED

### The Fundamental Principle

**Every minor key shares its key signature with a relative major key.**

**Why?**

Because the **natural minor scale** uses the same notes as its relative major, just starting from a different note.

**C Major:** C - D - E - F - G - A - B - C  
**A Natural Minor:** A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A

**Same notes = same key signature (no sharps or flats)**

**This relationship holds for ALL 12 keys.**

---

### Relative Major/Minor Pairs

**Key signature: No sharps, no flats**
- **C Major** / **A minor**

**Key signature: 1 sharp (F#)**
- **G Major** / **E minor**

**Key signature: 2 sharps (F#, C#)**
- **D Major** / **B minor**

**Key signature: 3 sharps (F#, C#, G#)**
- **A Major** / **F# minor**

**Key signature: 4 sharps (F#, C#, G#, D#)**
- **E Major** / **C# minor**

**And so on...**

**Every major key has a relative minor partner that shares the same key signature.**

---

### Finding the Relative Minor

**From major key ‚Üí relative minor:**

**Go DOWN 3 half steps (minor 3rd) from the major tonic.**

**Examples:**

**C major ‚Üí Count down 3 half steps:**  
C ‚Üí B (1) ‚Üí Bb (2) ‚Üí A (3)  
**Relative minor: A minor**

**G major ‚Üí Count down 3 half steps:**  
G ‚Üí F# (1) ‚Üí F (2) ‚Üí E (3)  
**Relative minor: E minor**

**D major ‚Üí Count down 3 half steps:**  
D ‚Üí C# (1) ‚Üí C (2) ‚Üí B (3)  
**Relative minor: B minor**

---

**Or use the scale degree method (easier):**

**Relative minor = 6th scale degree of the major scale**

**C major scale:** C - D - E - F - G - **A** - B - C  
6th degree = A  
**Relative minor: A minor**

**G major scale:** G - A - B - C - D - **E** - F# - G  
6th degree = E  
**Relative minor: E minor**

---

### Finding the Relative Major

**From minor key ‚Üí relative major:**

**Go UP 3 half steps (minor 3rd) from the minor tonic.**

**Examples:**

**A minor ‚Üí Count up 3 half steps:**  
A ‚Üí Bb (1) ‚Üí B (2) ‚Üí C (3)  
**Relative major: C major**

**E minor ‚Üí Count up 3 half steps:**  
E ‚Üí F (1) ‚Üí F# (2) ‚Üí G (3)  
**Relative major: G major**

**B minor ‚Üí Count up 3 half steps:**  
B ‚Üí C (1) ‚Üí C# (2) ‚Üí D (3)  
**Relative major: D major**

## üìñ SECTION 2: ALL 12 MINOR KEY SIGNATURES

### Sharp Minor Keys

**Order of sharps (review): F - C - G - D - A - E - B**

---

**E Minor (1 sharp: F#)**
- Relative major: G major
- Scale: E - F# - G - A - B - C - D - E
- Key signature: F# (same as G major)

**B Minor (2 sharps: F#, C#)**
- Relative major: D major
- Scale: B - C# - D - E - F# - G - A - B
- Key signature: F#, C# (same as D major)

**F# Minor (3 sharps: F#, C#, G#)**
- Relative major: A major
- Scale: F# - G# - A - B - C# - D - E - F#
- Key signature: F#, C#, G# (same as A major)

**C# Minor (4 sharps: F#, C#, G#, D#)**
- Relative major: E major
- Scale: C# - D# - E - F# - G# - A - B - C#
- Key signature: F#, C#, G#, D# (same as E major)

**G# Minor (5 sharps: F#, C#, G#, D#, A#)**
- Relative major: B major
- Scale: G# - A# - B - C# - D# - E - F# - G#
- Key signature: F#, C#, G#, D#, A# (same as B major)

**D# Minor (6 sharps: F#, C#, G#, D#, A#, E#)**
- Relative major: F# major
- Scale: D# - E# - F# - G# - A# - B - C# - D#
- Key signature: All sharps except B# (same as F# major)

**A# Minor (7 sharps: all notes sharp)**
- Relative major: C# major
- Rarely used (prefer Bb minor)

---

### Flat Minor Keys

**Order of flats (review): B - E - A - D - G - C - F**

---

**D Minor (1 flat: Bb)**
- Relative major: F major
- Scale: D - E - F - G - A - Bb - C - D
- Key signature: Bb (same as F major)

**G Minor (2 flats: Bb, Eb)**
- Relative major: Bb major
- Scale: G - A - Bb - C - D - Eb - F - G
- Key signature: Bb, Eb (same as Bb major)

**C Minor (3 flats: Bb, Eb, Ab)**
- Relative major: Eb major
- Scale: C - D - Eb - F - G - Ab - Bb - C
- Key signature: Bb, Eb, Ab (same as Eb major)

**F Minor (4 flats: Bb, Eb, Ab, Db)**
- Relative major: Ab major
- Scale: F - G - Ab - Bb - C - Db - Eb - F
- Key signature: Bb, Eb, Ab, Db (same as Ab major)

**Bb Minor (5 flats: Bb, Eb, Ab, Db, Gb)**
- Relative major: Db major
- Scale: Bb - C - Db - Eb - F - Gb - Ab - Bb
- Key signature: Bb, Eb, Ab, Db, Gb (same as Db major)

**Eb Minor (6 flats: Bb, Eb, Ab, Db, Gb, Cb)**
- Relative major: Gb major
- Scale: Eb - F - Gb - Ab - Bb - Cb - Db - Eb
- Key signature: All flats except Fb (same as Gb major)

**Ab Minor (7 flats: all notes flat)**
- Relative major: Cb major
- Rarely used (prefer G# minor)

---

### Quick Reference: All Minor Keys

**Sharp Keys:**
- 0 sharps: **A minor** (rel. to C major)
- 1 sharp: **E minor** (rel. to G major)
- 2 sharps: **B minor** (rel. to D major)
- 3 sharps: **F# minor** (rel. to A major)
- 4 sharps: **C# minor** (rel. to E major)
- 5 sharps: **G# minor** (rel. to B major)

**Flat Keys:**
- 1 flat: **D minor** (rel. to F major)
- 2 flats: **G minor** (rel. to Bb major)
- 3 flats: **C minor** (rel. to Eb major)
- 4 flats: **F minor** (rel. to Ab major)
- 5 flats: **Bb minor** (rel. to Db major)

## üìñ SECTION 3: THE CIRCLE OF FIFTHS FOR MINOR KEYS

### The Complete Circle (Major + Minor)

**The Circle of Fifths includes BOTH major and minor keys:**

```
              C / Am
             ‚ñí       ‚ñì
        F / Dm         G / Em
        ‚ñí               ‚ñì
   Bb / Gm              D / Bm
     ‚ñí                   ‚ñì
Eb / Cm                  A / F#m
   ‚ñí                       ‚ñì
Ab / Fm                    E / C#m
   ‚ñì                       ‚ñí
Db / Bbm                  B / G#m
     ‚ñì                   ‚ñí
   Gb / Ebm         F# / D#m
        ‚ñì           ‚ñí
     Cb / Abm = B / G#m

Outer circle: MAJOR KEYS
Inner circle: MINOR KEYS
```

**Each position on the circle has TWO keys:**
- **Outer:** Major key
- **Inner:** Relative minor (shares same key signature)

---

### Moving Around the Circle (Minor Keys)

**Clockwise (adding sharps):**

**A minor (0)** ‚Üí **E minor (1‚ôØ)** ‚Üí **B minor (2‚ôØ)** ‚Üí **F# minor (3‚ôØ)** ‚Üí **C# minor (4‚ôØ)** ‚Üí **G# minor (5‚ôØ)** ‚Üí **D# minor (6‚ôØ)** ‚Üí **A# minor (7‚ôØ)**

Each step clockwise adds one sharp (same order: F-C-G-D-A-E-B)

---

**Counter-clockwise (adding flats):**

**A minor (0)** ‚Üí **D minor (1‚ô≠)** ‚Üí **G minor (2‚ô≠)** ‚Üí **C minor (3‚ô≠)** ‚Üí **F minor (4‚ô≠)** ‚Üí **Bb minor (5‚ô≠)** ‚Üí **Eb minor (6‚ô≠)** ‚Üí **Ab minor (7‚ô≠)**

Each step counter-clockwise adds one flat (same order: B-E-A-D-G-C-F)

---

### Identifying Major vs. Minor From Key Signature

**Problem:** A key signature tells you the sharps/flats, but not whether it's major or minor.

**Example:** 1 sharp (F#) = G major OR E minor

**How to tell which?**

**Look at the music:**

1. **What's the first chord?** (Usually the tonic)
   - If it's G major ‚Üí probably G major key
   - If it's E minor ‚Üí probably E minor key

2. **What's the last chord?** (Usually resolves to tonic)
   - Ends on G major ‚Üí G major key
   - Ends on E minor ‚Üí E minor key

3. **What accidentals appear?** (Altered notes)
   - If you see D# often ‚Üí probably E harmonic minor (raised 7th)
   - If no accidentals ‚Üí could be either (look at chords)

4. **Overall mood/sound**
   - Bright, happy ‚Üí major key
   - Dark, sad ‚Üí minor key

**In sheet music, composers often write "in E minor" to avoid ambiguity.**

## üìñ SECTION 4: TRANSPOSING TO MINOR KEYS

### Transposing a Minor Key Song

**Task: Transpose "House of the Rising Sun" (A minor) to D minor**

**Original key: A minor**
- Key signature: No sharps or flats (same as C major)
- Chords: Am - C - D - F - Am - E - Am - E

---

**New key: D minor**
- Key signature: 1 flat (Bb) - same as F major
- Relative major: F major

**Transposition method: Interval-based**

A minor ‚Üí D minor = up a perfect 4th (or down a perfect 5th)

**Apply the same interval to each chord:**

**Original (A minor):**
- Am ‚Üí Dm (up a 4th)
- C ‚Üí F (up a 4th)
- D ‚Üí G (up a 4th)
- F ‚Üí Bb (up a 4th)
- E ‚Üí A (up a 4th)

**Result in D minor:**
**Dm - F - G - Bb - Dm - A - Dm - A**

---

**Or use Roman numerals (more reliable):**

**Analyze original progression in A minor:**
- Am = i (tonic minor)
- C = III (relative major)
- D = IV (subdominant)
- F = VI (submediant)
- E = V (dominant)

**Apply Roman numerals in D minor:**
- i = Dm
- III = F
- IV = G
- VI = Bb
- V = A

**Same result: Dm - F - G - Bb - Dm - A - Dm - A**

## üìñ SECTION 5: HARMONIC & MELODIC MINOR KEY SIGNATURES

### Important Note About Key Signatures

**Key signatures show ONLY the natural minor scale.**

**Harmonic and melodic minor use accidentals** (‚ôØ, ‚ôÆ, ‚ô≠ symbols in the music).

---

**Example: A Minor Key Signature**

**Key signature:** No sharps or flats

**In the music:**
- **A natural minor:** Uses no accidentals (A-B-C-D-E-F-G-A)
- **A harmonic minor:** Adds G# as an accidental (raised 7th)
- **A melodic minor:** Adds F# and G# as accidentals (raised 6th & 7th ascending)

**The key signature doesn't change for harmonic or melodic minor.**  
**The raised notes appear as accidentals in the actual music.**

---

**Example: E Minor Key Signature**

**Key signature:** 1 sharp (F#)

**In the music:**
- **E natural minor:** Uses F# from key signature (E-F#-G-A-B-C-D-E)
- **E harmonic minor:** Adds D# as accidental (raised 7th)
- **E melodic minor:** Adds C# and D# as accidentals (raised 6th & 7th ascending)

**The composer writes the accidentals where needed in the score.**
