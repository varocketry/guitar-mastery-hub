### What Is a "Chord Shape"?

**A chord shape** = a physical finger pattern on the fretboard

**Here's the revolutionary insight:**

The same finger pattern (shape) can be **moved to different frets** to create different chords!

**Example: The E-Shape**
```
When you play E major (you'll learn this in Lesson 6):
- 3 fingers
- Specific strings
- Specific frets
- Forms a visual pattern

That SAME finger pattern:
- At open position = E major
- At fret 1 (with bar) = F major
- At fret 3 (with bar) = G major
- At fret 5 (with bar) = A major
- At fret 7 (with bar) = B major

ONE shape = UNLIMITED chords!
```

**This is what Den Lopez teaches: Learn the shape once, use it everywhere.**

---

### The 6 Core Shapes (Den Lopez System)

**Every chord on guitar comes from one of 6 basic shapes**:

```
1. E-SHAPE FAMILY
   - Open E minor (Em - Lesson 1, you know this!)
   - Open E major (Lesson 6, coming soon)
   - Movable E-shape barre chords (Lesson 21)
   
   Den Lopez calls this the "most important shape" 
   because it's the easiest to move and most versatile.

2. A-SHAPE FAMILY  
   - Open A minor (Am - Lesson 1, you know this!)
   - Open A major (Lesson 8)
   - Movable A-shape barre chords (Lesson 22)
   
   Second most important shape - complements E-shape perfectly.

3. D-SHAPE
   - Open D major (Lesson 2, you know this!)
   - Movable D-shape (rare, not covered until advanced lessons)
   
   Usually played in open position, but the shape still matters.

4. C-SHAPE
   - Open C major (Lesson 3, you know this!)
   - Movable C-shape (rare, not covered until advanced lessons)
   
   Like D-shape, usually stays in open position for beginners.

5. G-SHAPE
   - Open G major (Lesson 3, you know this!)
   - Movable G-shape (rare, not covered until advanced lessons)
   
   Great open chord, less commonly moved around.

6. TRIAD SHAPES
   - Compact 3-note voicings
   - Very movable
   - You'll learn these in intermediate lessons
```

**You already know 5 of the 6 core shapes!** You just didn't know they were "shapes" yet.
