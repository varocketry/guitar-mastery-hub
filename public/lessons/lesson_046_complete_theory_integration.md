## üìñ SECTION 2: COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS EXERCISES

### Analysis Exercise 1: "Let It Be" - The Beatles

**Key: C Major**

**Verse progression: C - G - Am - F**

**Complete analysis:**

**Scale:** C-D-E-F-G-A-B-C (no sharps/flats)

**Diatonic chords:**
- I=C, ii=Dm, iii=Em, IV=F, V=G, vi=Am, vii¬∞=Bdim

**Progression analysis:**
- C = I (Tonic, home, stable)
- G = V (Dominant, creates tension)
- Am = vi (Submediant, relative minor, emotional depth)
- F = IV (Subdominant, pre-dominant function)

**Roman numerals: I - V - vi - IV**

**Harmonic function:**
```
Tonic ‚Üí Dominant ‚Üí Relative Minor ‚Üí Subdominant
Home  ‚Üí Tension  ‚Üí Emotion        ‚Üí Return motion
```

**Why it works:**
- Starts strong (I)
- Creates tension (V)
- Adds emotional depth (vi = relative minor)
- Sets up return to I (IV subdominant function)
- **One of the most popular progressions in music**

**Voice leading (with inversions for smoothness):**
- C - **G/B** - Am - F (bass: C-B-A-F, descending)

**This progression appears in thousands of songs.**

### Analysis Exercise 2: "Stairway to Heaven" - Led Zeppelin

**Key: A minor (primarily)**

**Intro progression (simplified): Am - E/G# - C/G - D/F# - Fmaj7 - G - Am**

**Complete analysis:**

**Scale (A natural minor):** A-B-C-D-E-F-G-A

**Diatonic chords:**
- i=Am, ii¬∞=Bdim, III=C, iv=Dm, v=Em, VI=F, VII=G

**Progression analysis:**
- Am = i (Tonic minor, dark home)
- E/G# = V/3 (Harmonic minor V in first inversion!)
- C/G = III/5 (Relative major in second inversion)
- D/F# = IV/3 (Borrowed from A major! Modal interchange)
- Fmaj7 = VImaj7 (Submediant with color)
- G = VII (Subtonic major, natural minor)
- Am = i (Return to tonic)

**Why it's sophisticated:**
- Uses harmonic minor (E major, not Em)
- Borrows from parallel major (D major)
- Extensive use of inversions (descending bass line)
- **Mixes natural and harmonic minor**
- **Uses modal interchange**

**This is advanced theory in practice.**

### Analysis Exercise 3: "Creep" - Radiohead

**Key: G Major**

**Progression: G - B - C - Cm**

**Complete analysis:**

**Scale (G major):** G-A-B-C-D-E-F#-G

**Diatonic chords:**
- I=G, ii=Am, iii=Bm, IV=C, V=D, vi=Em, vii¬∞=F#dim

**Progression analysis:**
- G = I (Tonic major)
- **B = III** (MAJOR mediant - not diatonic! Borrowed from G minor or secondary dominant?)
- C = IV (Subdominant major)
- **Cm = iv** (Minor subdominant - BORROWED from G minor!)

**Roman numerals: I - III - IV - iv**

**Why it's dramatic:**
- III (B major) is unexpected (should be iii/Bm in G major)
- **Cm is modal interchange** (borrowed iv from G minor)
- Creates dark, haunting quality
- **The borrowed iv IS the song's signature**

**Without that Cm, "Creep" wouldn't be "Creep."**

**This is modal interchange creating emotional power.**
